so i want to talk about this layout a bit overall

i built the part from 1:10 all the way to the second chorus originally and i wasn't sure if i was going to even do anything past that, as i really just wanted to build older-style locked camera gameplay that focused on playability, fun, and a little bit of unconventional movement such as the locked floor and ceiling clicks etc.
this was admittedly the part i had the most fun with, and building the intro much later (up until the ship at least). the pre-chorus ship was subject to change, and that's why it looks and plays horrible. another thing to mention is i didn't plan on adding anything more than just little structuring and moving object gimmicks in some sections. i wanted the gameplay to speak for itself

as you can hear from the original description, the moving object bug was a nightmare. i personally do not understand how every time i try to make a gimmick (which is like, yknow almost ALL the time if i'm given the leeway to do so) it ends up costing me multiple hours finding a new geometry dash bug. i'm not going to blame the reason i didn't finish this on that, but it definitely comepletely shot my motivation to finish the final section.

on that note, i want to say that the 2nd half was at least gearing up to be more interesting than the first. my original idea of each side of the "boxed off" moving object gimmick area coming in from the top and exiting back up on the top of the screen (almost like a funnel on both top corners? idk) just didn't end up being feasible to create playable gameplay or making the gimmick work properly due to how i would basically have to "rotate" the blocks around the edges of the screen. this probably makes sense to nobody but i just wanted to put it out there. beyond that, the next few sections were all going to split the duals on the x axis in some way (i was maybe even thinking about going vertical but probably not). it's sad that i never pulled together the motivation to actually do it, but with the amount of looming bugfixing that i always feel inevitably awaits me, i would always put off any kind of motivation (which in this case is really just depression or anger, the power of most of my solos, but especially this one) and just never actually open the level. i would do just about anything else.

my final point, and the main reason i wanted this behind another "internet wall," is that this layout in every phase of it's building was carrying a mental story. it admittedly didn't start out that way, as the section i began building the layout with (that i mentioned at the start that i liked) was just a small depressive episode that i worked through like normal and was able to get some fun stuff built as a result. however, during the summer of 2025 i went through a really really depressing experience where i let down a few people, and when i got the chance to let out my depression and frustration, i built a good chunk of the rest of the layout, hoping to soon finish it, and dedicate it to the people that i let down. the problem is that this mental tie became the entire identity of the layout in my head. so when i wanted to work on the layout more, not only was i thinking about how hard it would be to make it good, but i would essentially relive my incredibly frustrating summer experience all over again mentally.

i'm definitely just being dramatic about this. but that's why i'm honestly glad that this is, to me, completely dead, despite the work i put into it. the layout was dedicated to that small group of people as an embodiment of emotions that probably should've been used in a better way. if those people happen to understand what i mean and who they are, here you go i guess lmao
